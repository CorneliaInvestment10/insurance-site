Clients in good to excellent health may qualify for standard rate classifications and full immediate benefit coverage across a broad range of carriers and face amounts.

Federal Privacy Law
HIPAA Privacy Notice
What HIPAA Is

The Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 (HIPAA) is a federal law that establishes national standards for the protection of certain health information. HIPAA restricts how health information may be used and shared and gives individuals specific rights regarding their health data.

How It Applies Here

Any health-related information you provide through this website or during our advisory engagement is handled in strict compliance with applicable HIPAA privacy standards. This includes any medical history, conditions, medications, or other health information submitted as part of an intake questionnaire or consultation.

Your health information will not be disclosed to any insurance carrier or third party without your written authorization prior to any formal application submission. You will be informed of what information is being shared, with whom, and for what purpose before any such disclosure occurs.

Your HIPAA Rights
  • The right to receive a notice of privacy practices
  • The right to access and review health information held about you
  • The right to request corrections to inaccurate information
  • The right to request restrictions on certain uses or disclosures
  • The right to receive an accounting of disclosures made on your behalf
  • The right to file a complaint if you believe your privacy rights have been violated
If you believe your health information privacy rights have been violated, you may file a complaint with the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services Office for Civil Rights. Filing a complaint will not affect your ability to obtain coverage or services through this practice.
Industry Disclosure
MIB, FCRA, MVR & Pharmacy Records Disclosure
Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA)

In connection with a formal application for life insurance, the issuing insurance company may obtain a consumer report about you pursuant to the Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA). This report may include information about your credit history, character, general reputation, personal characteristics, or mode of living.

If the insurance company takes an adverse action based in whole or in part on information in a consumer report - such as declining coverage, charging a higher rate, or limiting the benefit offered - the company is required by law to notify you and to provide you with the name, address, and phone number of the consumer reporting agency that supplied the report. You then have the right to obtain a copy of that report and to dispute any information you believe to be inaccurate.

Medical Information Bureau (MIB)

Most life and health insurance companies participate in the Medical Information Bureau (MIB), a data exchange used by member companies to share coded information about prior insurance applications and claims. If you have previously applied for individually underwritten life or health insurance with a member company, the MIB may have a record on file.

The insurance company may request information from the MIB as part of the underwriting process. This exchange helps identify inconsistencies between applications and assists the industry in detecting misrepresentation, which helps keep premiums more affordable for all consumers. You have the right to request a copy of your MIB file directly from MIB at mib.com or by contacting MIB at 1-866-692-6901.

Pharmacy Records (Rx History)

Depending on the carrier and the product applied for, the insurance company may request a prescription history report through a prescription drug history database. This report pulls current and historical prescription drug records from participating pharmacies and is used to cross-reference health conditions disclosed on the application with medication history.

Prescription history checks are a standard component of underwriting for many life insurance products. You will be informed if such a check is requested and will be asked to authorize it as part of the formal application process.

Motor Vehicle Report (MVR)

Depending on the carrier and product, the insurance company may obtain a Motor Vehicle Report reviewing your driving history, including any violations, DUI convictions, or license suspensions. Driving history is considered a lifestyle and risk factor by some carriers in life insurance underwriting. An MVR may be requested as part of the formal application process, and you will be informed if it is required.

All reports listed above may be required depending on the specific carrier and product selected at the time of formal application. You will be informed of any inquiry before your application is submitted. No application will be submitted on your behalf without your explicit instruction and authorization.
