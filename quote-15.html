Before We Begin

One foundational question that determines how the application is structured.

The Insured

The insured is the individual whose life is covered under the policy. The death benefit is payable upon the death of the insured. Their age, gender, and health classification determine the applicable premium rate.

The Owner

The owner is the party who holds and controls the policy - responsible for premium payments and entitled to designate or modify beneficiaries. The owner and the insured may be the same individual or separate parties (e.g., a spouse insuring a partner, or a parent insuring a child).

Most common scenario: The applicant is insuring their own life and will pay their own premiums. In that case, the owner and insured are the same individual - select Yes. If you are applying to insure another person's life, select No.

What to Have Ready for Your Consultation

During your consultation, the contracted insurance company may require the following to process your application and draft premiums according to the payment frequency you selected:

  • - A valid government-issued photo ID
  • - Your Social Security Number (required by the issuing carrier for application processing)
  • - Your banking information for premium drafting - account and routing number (premiums are drafted solely by the contracted life insurance company following application approval, not by this advisory practice)
  • - A list of your current medications including prescription and over-the-counter supplements
  • - The full legal name, date of birth, and relationship of your intended beneficiary
  • - Your physician's name and contact information if applicable
Application Disclosures

Depending on the carrier and product selected during your consultation, the following reports may be required by the issuing insurance company as part of the underwriting process. These are standard industry practices and you will be informed of any inquiry before your application is submitted.

  • -FCRA - Fair Credit Reporting Act: A consumer report including credit, character, and lifestyle information may be requested depending on company and product selection.
  • -MIB - Medical Information Bureau: A review of prior insurance application history may be conducted depending on company and product selection.
  • -Pharmacy Records (Rx): A prescription drug history report such as Milliman IntelliScript may be requested depending on company and product selection.
  • -MVR - Motor Vehicle Report: A review of your driving history may be requested depending on company and product selection.
