Before We Begin

One foundational question that determines how the application is structured.

The Insured

The insured is the individual whose life is covered under the policy. The death benefit is payable upon the death of the insured. Their age, gender, and health classification determine the applicable premium rate.

The Owner

The owner is the party who holds and controls the policy — responsible for premium payments and entitled to designate or modify beneficiaries. The owner and the insured may be the same individual or separate parties (e.g., a spouse insuring a partner, or a parent insuring a child).

Most common scenario: The applicant is insuring their own life and will pay their own premiums. In that case, the owner and insured are the same individual — select Yes. If you are applying to insure another person's life, select No.

Section 7 — Optional Riders

Riders are optional provisions added to a base policy that expand or modify coverage. Select any you would like to be considered. Rider availability, terms, and cost vary by carrier and are subject to underwriting guidelines.

Waiver of Premium
A rider that waives the premium obligation if the insured becomes totally disabled and unable to maintain employment. The policy remains in force during the qualifying disability period without premium payment. Definitions of total disability and qualifying criteria vary among carriers.
Accidental Death Benefit (ADB)
A rider that provides an additional death benefit in the event the insured dies as a direct result of a covered accident. Does not apply to death resulting from illness or disease. Covered accidents, exclusions, and benefit amounts vary by carrier and policy form.
Living Benefits
A category of riders that allow the insured to access a portion of the death benefit while living upon the occurrence of a qualifying event — which may include terminal illness, chronic illness, or critical illness diagnosis. Qualifying conditions, acceleration amounts, and benefit structures differ among carriers. Any amount advanced reduces the remaining death benefit. Guidelines and trigger criteria are subject to change by the issuing carrier.
Term Rider
A rider that adds a term life insurance component to a base whole life policy, or extends additional coverage to eligible dependents such as children. Term riders may be convertible to permanent coverage at the end of the term period without new evidence of insurability, depending on carrier guidelines. Eligibility requirements and conversion provisions vary among carriers.
