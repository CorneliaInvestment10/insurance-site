Federal Privacy Law
HIPAA Privacy Notice
What HIPAA Is

The Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 (HIPAA) is a federal law that establishes national standards for the protection of certain health information. HIPAA restricts how health information may be used and shared and gives individuals specific rights regarding their health data.

How It Applies Here

Any health-related information you provide through this website or during our advisory engagement is handled in strict compliance with applicable HIPAA privacy standards. This includes any medical history, conditions, medications, or other health information submitted as part of an intake questionnaire or consultation.

Your health information will not be disclosed to any insurance carrier or third party without your written authorization prior to any formal application submission. You will be informed of what information is being shared, with whom, and for what purpose before any such disclosure occurs.

Your HIPAA Rights
  • The right to receive a notice of privacy practices
  • The right to access and review health information held about you
  • The right to request corrections to inaccurate information
  • The right to request restrictions on certain uses or disclosures
  • The right to receive an accounting of disclosures made on your behalf
  • The right to file a complaint if you believe your privacy rights have been violated
If you believe your health information privacy rights have been violated, you may file a complaint with the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services Office for Civil Rights. Filing a complaint will not affect your ability to obtain coverage or services through this practice.
Federal Consumer Protection Law
Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA)
What the FCRA Is

The Fair Credit Reporting Act is a federal law that regulates how consumer reporting agencies collect, use, and share information about individuals, including for insurance underwriting purposes.

How It Applies

In connection with a formal application for life insurance, the issuing carrier may obtain a consumer report about you. If any adverse action is taken based on that report, the carrier must notify you and provide the name of the reporting agency so you can request a copy and dispute inaccuracies.

No consumer report will be requested without your knowledge. You will be informed prior to any application submission.
Industry Disclosure
Medical Information Bureau (MIB)
What the MIB Is

The Medical Information Bureau (MIB) is a member-owned organization that operates as an information exchange among participating life and health insurance companies.

How It Affects Your Application

When you apply for individually underwritten life or health insurance, the issuing carrier may request coded information from the MIB regarding prior insurance applications. You have the right to request a copy of your MIB file at mib.com or by calling 1-866-692-6901.

A formal MIB inquiry will only occur in connection with a formal application submitted at your direction.
Underwriting Disclosure
Pharmacy Records (Rx History)
What This Is

Depending on the carrier and product applied for, the issuing company may request a report that includes your current and historical prescription drug records from participating pharmacies. This is a standard underwriting tool used by many life insurance carriers.

How It Is Used

Prescription drug history is reviewed to correlate medications on file with health conditions disclosed on the application. You will be informed if such a check is required before your application is submitted.

Pharmacy record checks may or may not be required depending on the specific carrier and product selected.
Underwriting Disclosure
Motor Vehicle Report (MVR)
What an MVR Is

A Motor Vehicle Report provides a history of your driving record including traffic violations, license suspensions, DUI or DWI convictions, and the current status of your driver's license.

How It Is Used

Some life insurance carriers review driving history as part of underwriting. An MVR may be requested depending on the carrier's guidelines for the product applied for. You will be informed if one is required before your application is submitted.

An MVR may or may not be required depending on the specific carrier and product. You may obtain your own driving record directly from your state DMV at any time.
