Federal Consumer Protection Law
Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA)
What the FCRA Is

The Fair Credit Reporting Act is a federal law that regulates how consumer reporting agencies collect, use, and share information about individuals. It gives consumers specific rights when their information is used to make decisions about them, including decisions related to insurance.

How It Applies to Your Application

In connection with a formal application for life insurance, the issuing insurance company may obtain a consumer report about you. This report can include information about your credit standing, character, general reputation, personal characteristics, or mode of living.

If the insurance company takes an adverse action based on information in a consumer report, such as declining your application or charging a higher rate, they are required by law to notify you and provide the name and address of the reporting agency so you can request a copy and dispute any inaccuracies.

No consumer report will be requested on your behalf without your knowledge. You will be informed prior to any application submission.
Industry Disclosure
Medical Information Bureau (MIB)
What the MIB Is

The Medical Information Bureau is a member-owned organization that operates as an information exchange among participating life and health insurance companies. Most individually underwritten life and health insurance carriers are MIB members.

How It Affects Your Application

When you apply for individually underwritten life or health insurance, the issuing carrier may request information from the MIB about prior insurance applications or claims you may have submitted with other member companies. You have the right to request a copy of your MIB file directly from MIB at mib.com or by calling 1-866-692-6901.

A formal MIB inquiry will not be initiated without your knowledge and will only occur in connection with a formal application submitted at your direction.
Underwriting Disclosure
Pharmacy Records (Rx History)
What a Pharmacy Records Check Is

Depending on the insurance carrier and the product applied for, the issuing company may request a report that includes your current and historical prescription drug records from participating pharmacies. This is a standard underwriting tool used by many life insurance carriers.

How It Is Used

Prescription drug history is reviewed during underwriting to help the carrier correlate medications on file with health conditions disclosed on the application. You will be informed if such a check is required before your application is submitted.

Pharmacy record checks may or may not be required depending on the specific carrier and product. Not every carrier uses this report for every application.
Underwriting Disclosure
Motor Vehicle Report (MVR)
What a Motor Vehicle Report Is

A Motor Vehicle Report provides a history of your driving record including traffic violations, license suspensions, DUI or DWI convictions, and the current status of your driver's license.

How It Is Used

Some life insurance carriers review driving history as part of underwriting because certain driving patterns are considered risk indicators. An MVR may be requested depending on the carrier's guidelines for the specific product applied for. You will be informed if one is required before your application is submitted.

An MVR may or may not be required depending on the specific carrier and product. You may obtain a copy of your own driving record directly from your state DMV at any time.
