Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA)
▼

In connection with a formal application for life insurance, the issuing insurance company may obtain a consumer report about you pursuant to the Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA). This report may include information about your credit history, character, general reputation, personal characteristics, or mode of living. If the insurance company takes an adverse action based in whole or in part on information in a consumer report, the company is required by law to notify you and provide the name, address, and phone number of the consumer reporting agency that supplied the report. You then have the right to obtain a copy of that report and dispute any information you believe to be inaccurate. No consumer report will be requested on your behalf without your knowledge and only in connection with a formal application submitted at your direction.

Medical Information Bureau (MIB)
▼

Most life and health insurance companies participate in the Medical Information Bureau (MIB), a data exchange used by member companies to share coded information about prior insurance applications and claims. If you have previously applied for individually underwritten life or health insurance with a member company, the MIB may have a record on file. The insurance company may request information from the MIB as part of the underwriting process. You have the right to request a copy of your MIB file directly from MIB at mib.com or by calling 1-866-692-6901. A formal MIB inquiry will not be initiated without your knowledge and will only occur in connection with a formal application submitted at your direction.

Pharmacy Records (Rx History)
▼

Depending on the insurance carrier and the product applied for, the issuing company may request a report that includes your current and historical prescription drug records from participating pharmacies. This type of report is a standard underwriting tool used by many life insurance carriers. Prescription drug history is reviewed during underwriting to correlate medications on file with health conditions disclosed on the application. You will be informed if such a check is required before your application is submitted, and you will be asked to authorize it as part of the formal application process. A prescription drug history report may or may not be required depending on the specific carrier and product selected.

Motor Vehicle Report (MVR)
▼

Depending on the carrier and product, the insurance company may obtain a Motor Vehicle Report reviewing your driving history, including any violations, DUI or DWI convictions, or license suspensions. Driving history is considered a lifestyle and risk factor by some carriers in life insurance underwriting. An MVR may be requested as part of the formal application process. You will be informed if it is required before your application is submitted. An MVR may or may not be required depending on the specific carrier and product. You may obtain a copy of your own driving record directly from your state Department of Motor Vehicles at any time.
